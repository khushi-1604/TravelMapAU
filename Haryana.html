SHRI DEVIKOOP BHADRAKALI SHAKTIPEETH TEMPLE , KURUKESHTRA

Shri Devikoop Bhadrakali Shaktipeeth Temple in Kurukshetra is believed to be one of the 51 Shaktipeeths, where the ankle of Goddess Sati is said to have fallen. The temple's origins are rooted in the ancient legend of Sati and Lord Shiva, where parts of Sati's body were scattered across India, creating sacred sites. It is a revered spot in Hindu mythology and holds great spiritual significance for devotees. The temple is also associated with the Mahabharata, as it is said that the Pandavas worshipped Goddess Bhadrakali here before the great battle.

Timings

Darshan : 6 am to 8 pm
Aarti : 5.15 am , 7.15 pm

AMBA BHAVANI TEMPLE,AMBALA

The Ambala Temple in Haryana is believed to have ancient origins, tracing back to the times when the region was a significant center for spiritual and religious activities. The temple is dedicated to the worship of various deities and has been a revered site for pilgrims for centuries. Over time, the temple has seen various renovations, with local legends and folklore adding to its sacred significance. Its location at the crossroads of ancient trade routes further enhanced its prominence in the region.

Timings

Darshan : 6 am to 12 pm , 1 pm to 9 pm
Aarti : 8 am to 6 pm
Temple close : 12 pm to 1 pm

SHRI MATA SHEETLA DEVI MANDIR

Shri Mata Sheetla Devi Mandir in Gurgaon, Haryana, is dedicated to Mata Sheetla, a revered goddess associated with curing smallpox and other diseases. The temple's origins date back to ancient times when the area was known as Guru Gram, named after Guru Dronacharya from the Mahabharata. The temple was built to honor Mata Sheetla, who is believed to have blessed the region with health and prosperity. Over time, it has become a significant pilgrimage site, attracting devotees seeking blessings for health and well-being.

Timings

Darshan : 6 am to 8 pm
No entry fee
