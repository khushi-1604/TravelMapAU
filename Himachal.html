HADIMBA TEMPLE

The Hadimba Temple, located in Manali, Himachal Pradesh, was built in 1553. It is dedicated to Hidimba Devi, a mythical character from the Mahabharata who was the wife of Bhima and the mother of Ghatotkacha. The temple was constructed by Raja Bahadur Singh and features traditional Himachali wooden architecture. Nestled amidst dense deodar forests, the temple holds significant religious and cultural importance.

Timings

Darshan : 8 am to 6 pm
No entry fee

MANIKARAN SAHIB

Manikaran Sahib, nestled in the Parvati Valley of Himachal Pradesh, holds deep spiritual significance. According to Sikh tradition, Guru Nanak Dev Ji visited this site with his disciple Bhai Mardana. The legend says that Bhai Mardana was hungry, but there was no food to cook. Guru Nanak Ji then asked him to lift a stone, revealing a hot spring where they cooked the food. Since then, the site became a revered pilgrimage, with the temple and Gurudwara dedicated to Guru Nanak's miracle.

CHAMUNDA DEVI

Chamunda Devi Temple, located in Himachal Pradesh, is an ancient shrine dedicated to the goddess Chamunda, a form of Durga. The temple's origins date back over 700 years when it was believed to have been established by a local king who had a divine vision of the goddess. The legend says that Goddess Chamunda killed the demons Chanda and Munda at this very spot, making it a sacred site. Over the centuries, the temple has become a significant pilgrimage destination, revered for its spiritual power and historical importance.

Timings

Darshan : 6 am to 9 pm
Aarti : 8 am , 6 pm
Temple close : 12 pm to 1 pm

JAKHU TEMPLE

Jakhu Temple, perched atop Jakhu Hill in Shimla, Himachal Pradesh, has ancient roots tied to the Hindu epic Ramayana. It is believed that Lord Hanuman stopped at this site while on his way to fetch the Sanjeevani herb to revive Lakshman during the battle of Lanka. The temple's location is said to be where Hanuman rested and left his footprints, marking it as a sacred spot. In honor of this divine event, the Jakhu Temple was later established, becoming a revered pilgrimage site. Today, it stands not only as a religious symbol but also as a testament to the mythological significance of the region.
