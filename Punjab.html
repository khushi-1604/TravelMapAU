GOLDEN TEMPLE

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, was founded in 1577 by Guru Ram Das, the fourth Sikh Guru, in Amritsar, Punjab. The construction of the temple was completed in 1604 under the leadership of the fifth Guru, Guru Arjan Dev, who also installed the Adi Granth, the holy scripture of Sikhism, inside the temple. The temple was built as a place of worship for people of all religions, reflecting the Sikh principles of equality and humility. The surrounding Amrit Sarovar (sacred pool) was believed to have healing powers, attracting devotees from all over. Over time, the temple has undergone several renovations, including being covered in gold leaf in the early 19th century by Maharaja Ranjit Singh, which gave it the iconic appearance it has today.
